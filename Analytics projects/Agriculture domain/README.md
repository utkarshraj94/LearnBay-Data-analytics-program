# Agricultural Productivity Analysis

## Objective

Analyze state-wise and season-wise crop production across India to measure regional productivity, track how production has evolved over time, and surface high-potential and underperforming crops — supporting data-driven decisions on resource allocation, planting schedules, and agricultural policy.

## Dataset

- **Source:** [`Crop_prod_study.csv`](Crop_prod_study.csv)
- **Size:** 246,091 records, 7 columns — `State_Name`, `District_Name`, `Crop_Year`, `Season`, `Crop`, `Area`, `Production`.
- **Coverage:** 33 states/UTs, 124 crop types, crop years 1997–2015.

## Key Insights

- **Kerala dominates total production**, well ahead of Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh, and Assam — driven almost entirely by coconut, a perennial, high-volume crop.
- **Coconut and Sugarcane are the two largest crops nationally** by total production, followed by Rice, Wheat, and Potato.
- **"Whole Year" season crops account for the majority of total production**, with Kharif (monsoon) contributing more than Rabi (winter), and Winter/Summer/Autumn seasons contributing comparatively little.
- Southern states (Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu) lead in production efficiency — a pattern other states could study and adapt.
- Wheat and rice remain the staple-scale crops best positioned for expansion to meet food security demand in the northwest and high-rainfall northeast respectively.
